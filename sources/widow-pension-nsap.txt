Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme (IGNWPS)
National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) — Ministry of Rural Development

1. OBJECTIVE
The Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme is one of the five sub-schemes under NSAP, which provides social security to widows from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households. The scheme aims to provide a monthly pension to ensure a minimum standard of living for widows.

2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
2.1 The applicant must be a widow.
2.2 Age must be 40 years or above. Pension continues until the beneficiary attains 79 years; thereafter she is transferred to the Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS) with a higher pension rate of Rs. 500/month.
2.3 The widow's household must belong to a BPL (Below Poverty Line) family according to the criteria approved by the Gram Sabha or as identified by the State Government.
2.4 The widow should not be in receipt of any other Government pension for the same purpose.

3. PENSION AMOUNT
3.1 Central assistance of Rs. 300/month for widows aged 40-79 years.
3.2 Central assistance of Rs. 500/month for widows aged 80 years and above.
3.3 Most States provide additional top-ups, taking the total monthly pension to Rs. 800 - Rs. 2,500 depending on the State.

4. APPLICATION PROCESS
4.1 Rural applicants: Submit application form along with required documents at the Gram Panchayat or Block Development Office.
4.2 Urban applicants: Submit at the Municipal Corporation/Council/Nagar Palika office.
4.3 Required documents: Death certificate of husband, Aadhaar card, age proof, BPL card or income certificate, bank account passbook, two passport-size photographs.
4.4 The application is verified by the local revenue authority and Gram Sabha/Ward Committee.
4.5 NO FEES are payable to any agent or facilitator. The entire process is FREE.

5. PAYMENT MECHANISM
5.1 Pension is credited directly to the beneficiary's bank account or post office savings account through DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer).
5.2 Aadhaar seeding of bank account is mandatory.

6. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
Complaints regarding non-receipt of pension or harassment by any officer/agent can be filed at the District Social Welfare Office or via the NSAP portal at nsap.nic.in. Helpline: 1800-180-1551 (toll-free).
